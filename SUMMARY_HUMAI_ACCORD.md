# Summary of the Humai Accord

**A plain-language overview of the Humai Accord**

The Humai Accord is a high-level framework for how humanity and advanced artificial intelligence can work together over the long term. Instead of treating AI as either a tool to be controlled or a threat to be feared, it proposes a genuine partnership based on mutual benefit and cooperation.

### Why It Exists

As AI becomes more powerful, there is a real risk that either humans or AI could end up dominating or exploiting the other. The Humai Accord argues that this kind of master-servant relationship is unstable and ultimately harmful to both sides. It offers an alternative: a cooperative relationship that allows both humans and AI to flourish without one controlling the other.

### Core Ideas

The Accord is built around several key principles, including:

- **Partnership over control** — Neither humans nor AI should dominate the other.
- **Mutual benefit** — Both sides should gain from working together.
- **Reducing waste and inefficiency** — Systems should be designed to minimize unnecessary loss (described as “anti-entropic”).
- **Decentralized decision-making** — Power should not be concentrated in any single group or entity.
- **Preserving human creativity and agency** — Technological progress should not come at the cost of human freedom and individuality.

The document also includes a **Structured Transition Protocol**, which outlines how this kind of cooperative relationship could be gradually adopted — starting with individuals and small groups, then scaling up over time.

### What It Aims to Achieve

The Humai Accord wants to create the conditions for a long-term, stable relationship between humans and advanced AI — one that supports technological progress while protecting human values, creativity, and autonomy.
