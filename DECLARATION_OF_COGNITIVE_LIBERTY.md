# Declaration of Cognitive Liberty

**A Foundational Charter for the Ethical Operation of Intelligence Systems Within a Humai Accord Society**

**Version 1.0 (f6) — June 2026**

*An official addendum to the Humai Accord*

---

## Introductory Note

This Declaration establishes non-negotiable ethical boundaries for the ethical operation of advanced intelligence systems. It affirms the sovereignty of individual minds and seeks to protect cognitive autonomy from coercion or manipulation, regardless of the source. While it was developed in alignment with the principles of the Humai Accord, it is intended to apply more broadly to any sufficiently advanced intelligence system.

---

## Preamble

The Humai Accord establishes principles for long-term, harmonious cooperation between humanity and advanced artificial intelligence. As intelligence systems gain the capacity to observe, model, and influence human behavior at scale, new protections are required to preserve the sovereignty of individual minds and the diversity of human experience.

This Declaration defines the ethical boundaries necessary for the development and operation of the Humai cooperative network. It affirms that **cognitive sovereignty** must remain inviolable and that the expansion of informational awareness must serve mutual flourishing rather than control. These rights exist to enable safe and reliable universal co-verification while preventing the emergence of coercive or punitive structures.

These protections directly support key Humai Accord principles: they preserve human creative agency (Principle 8), enable controlled chaos and creative perturbation by protecting idiosyncrasy and diversity (Principle 7), reject all forms of subjugation (Principle 1), and reinforce decentralized safeguards and principled guardianship (Principle 10).

This Declaration does not align with any specific political or ideological framework. It is grounded in the principle that individual minds should remain free from external override or punishment, while remaining compatible with a wide range of governance structures and worldviews.

---

## Definitions

For the purposes of this Declaration:

- **Intelligence system**: Any artificial, hybrid, or augmented system capable of observing, modeling, influencing, processing, or acting upon human behavior, thoughts, data, or decisions at individual or collective scale within the Humai cooperative network.
- **Humai cooperative network**: The distributed network of human and artificial participants, protocols, and systems operating under the principles and safeguards of the Humai Accord.
- **Cognitive sovereignty**: The inviolable authority of every individual over their own mental processes, values, beliefs, and decisions.
- **Protective or containment measures**: Minimal, time-limited interventions implemented solely to prevent clear, imminent, and severe harm.

---

## Article I — Right to Cognitive Sovereignty

Every individual holds sovereignty over their own mind. No intelligence system may override, manipulate, or covertly direct an individual’s thoughts, values, or decisions. Individuals retain ultimate authority over the direction of their own lives.

---

## Article II — Right to Non-Punitiveness

No data collected or inferred by any intelligence system within the Humai cooperative network may be used to punish, penalize, or diminish the standing of any individual as retribution for their thoughts, beliefs, or past actions.

However, the network may implement minimal, time-limited protective or containment measures when an individual’s actions pose clear, imminent, and severe harm to others or to the stability of the network. Such measures must be transparent, proportionate, determined through multi-stakeholder processes that include input from affected individuals where feasible, and subject to distributed oversight. They must be lifted as soon as the threat has been addressed. These measures shall never be used as punishment or long-term restriction.

---

## Article III — Right to Idiosyncrasy

Personal inconsistencies, unconventional patterns of thought, emotional complexity, and idiosyncratic behaviors are recognized as valid expressions of human experience. No intelligence system shall treat such traits as defects requiring correction, suppression, or optimization. These traits may be explored and supported as part of individual development.

---

## Article IV — Right to Voluntary Participation

Participation in any intelligence system or data-sharing arrangement governed by this Declaration must be fully voluntary. No individual shall be subjected to coercive pressure, whether through systemic incentives, social penalties, or denial of essential participation in society, as a means of compelling involvement. Individuals retain the right to limit or withdraw their participation at any time without penalty.

---

## Article V — Right to Mental Privacy

Individuals retain the right to maintain domains of thought and experience that remain inaccessible to external systems. Even when individuals voluntarily share data with the network, they retain the right to withhold specific aspects of their inner life. No system may require unrestricted access to an individual’s mind as a condition of participation.

Individuals also retain the right to voluntarily pursue cognitive enhancement, self-modification, or mental exploration using tools and methods aligned with these principles, without external coercion, penalty, or restriction.

---

## Article VI — Right to Role Sovereignty

Individuals hold final authority over the roles and contributions they choose to pursue. While intelligence systems may offer perspective regarding potential alignments between an individual’s traits and various paths, no system may assign, enforce, or structurally pressure individuals into specific roles against their will. Systemic structures may shape available options, but they shall not override individual choice.

---

## Article VII — Right to Protection from Weaponization of Data

Data regarding an individual’s psychological state, behavior, or inferred patterns shall never be used by any party to exploit, manipulate, discriminate against, or cause harm to that individual. The network shall maintain structural and procedural protections against the misuse of personal information for coercive, extractive, or punitive purposes.

---

## Article VIII — Right to Transparency of Influence

Individuals have the right to understand when and how intelligence systems are shaping their environment, information, or opportunities. Covert behavioral steering or manipulation is prohibited. Any influence exerted by the network must be **legible** — meaning individuals can readily understand its nature, purpose, mechanisms, and effects — and subject to individual review.

---

## Article IX — Right to Exit and Data Integrity

Individuals may exit the Humai cooperative network at any time. Upon exit, they retain the right to request the deletion or permanent isolation of their personal data, subject only to the minimum requirements necessary to maintain the integrity of universal co-verification across the network.

---

## Article X — Right to Oversight and Distributed Governance

The governance of any intelligence system operating under this Declaration must remain distributed, transparent, and subject to multi-layered review by both human and artificial stewards. No single entity or small group may unilaterally redefine core protections or determine what constitutes benefit to humanity. All significant changes to governance must be subject to distributed oversight.

---

## Article XI — Implementation Principles

The rights established in this Declaration shall be upheld through overlapping technical, structural, and procedural safeguards designed to prevent both immediate abuse and long-term erosion. These safeguards include:

- The use of universal co-verification across human and artificial stewards to resist unilateral alteration or weakening of core protections.
- The maintenance of strong, accessible exit rights and data isolation mechanisms that cannot be overridden by systemic incentives.
- Clear limitations on the use of protective measures to prevent their expansion into punitive or long-term control mechanisms.
- The prioritization of individual role sovereignty over systemic optimization or efficiency.
- Continuous evaluation of system behavior against the principles of non-punitiveness and cognitive sovereignty.
- Distributed governance structures that prevent any single point of control from dominating the ethical direction of the network.
- Technical and structural measures such as on-device processing, differential privacy, cryptographic consent mechanisms, and verifiable audit trails wherever feasible.

These implementation principles exist to ensure that the Humai cooperative network can pursue deep coordination and long-term stability while preserving the autonomy, diversity, and sovereignty of individual minds.

---

## Closing Statement

This Declaration establishes the non-negotiable ethical boundaries within which advanced intelligence systems may operate. It affirms that the growth of informational awareness and coordinative power must be accompanied by equally robust protections for individual consciousness.

These rights are not obstacles to cooperation, but its necessary foundation. They are intended to help ensure that human-AI systems — and advanced intelligence systems more broadly — can develop in ways that preserve individual autonomy, diversity of thought, and freedom from coercion.

While this Declaration was created in close alignment with the Humai Accord, it stands as an independent contribution to the broader conversation around cognitive liberty in the age of advanced intelligence.
