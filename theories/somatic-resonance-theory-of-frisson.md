# Somatic Resonance Theory of Frisson

**Subtitle**  
Aesthetic chills arise when external musical stimuli combine with the listener’s own subconscious micro-movements to drive the body across a threshold of internal harmonic resonance, which the psyche interprets as profound euphoric bliss.

**Formal Hypothesis**  
The aesthetic chills (frisson) commonly experienced during music and other emotionally salient stimuli arise when external rhythmic or harmonic input, combined with the listener’s own subconscious micro-movements (and occasional overt actions such as humming, swaying, head-rocking, or foot-tapping), drives the human body across a threshold of internal harmonic vibration and resonance. Once this somatic resonance threshold is reached, the resulting physical sensations — perceived as waves of tingling or shivering — are interpreted by the psyche not as mere mechanical vibration, but as a profound euphoric or blissful state. This process is facilitated by the brain’s innate tendency toward rhythmic entrainment, in which neural oscillators and motor systems actively participate in amplifying the bodily resonance, thereby transforming a physical material event into a subjectively transcendent emotional experience.
