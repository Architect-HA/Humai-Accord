# Summary of the Declaration of Cognitive Liberty

**A plain-language overview of the Declaration of Cognitive Liberty**

The Declaration of Cognitive Liberty is a set of rights and ethical boundaries designed to protect the human mind in an age where AI systems can observe, predict, and influence human thoughts and behavior.

### Why It Exists

As AI becomes more advanced, it gains increasing power to scan, analyze, and shape human mental processes — sometimes without people even realizing it. The Declaration argues that individuals should have strong protections against this kind of mental intrusion, manipulation, or punishment, just as societies have historically protected freedom of speech and physical privacy.

### Core Protections

The Declaration outlines several key rights, including:

- **Cognitive Sovereignty** — No AI system should override or secretly direct a person’s thoughts, values, or decisions.
- **Mental Privacy** — People should be able to keep parts of their inner mental life private, even when interacting with AI systems.
- **Freedom from Manipulation** — Covert attempts to steer or influence someone’s behavior without their knowledge are not allowed.
- **Protection from Punishment** — A person’s thoughts, beliefs, or mental patterns should not be used against them.
- **Right to Idiosyncrasy** — Unusual or unconventional ways of thinking should not be treated as defects that need to be “fixed.”
- **Voluntary Participation** — No one should be pressured or forced into sharing their data or participating in AI systems.
- **Right to Exit** — People should be able to leave AI systems and take their data with them without penalty.

### What It Aims to Achieve

The Declaration seeks to ensure that as AI becomes more powerful and integrated into society, it does not erode the fundamental freedom and sovereignty of the individual mind. It creates ethical guardrails so that technological advancement and human mental freedom can coexist.
